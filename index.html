NPH Audit Data Collection Guide

Complete explanations for all 74 fields

Section 1: Patient Demographics

Hospital Number

The unique patient ID (e.g., MRN123456)

Why: Links all data to one patient

Age

Age in years at time of surgery

Why: NPH more common in elderly; age affects outcomes

Gender

Male/Female/Other

Why: Check for gender differences

Ethnicity

Patient's ethnic background

Why: Identify population differences

Post code

First part of postal code

Why: Geographic distribution and travel distance

Section 2: Referral & Initial Assessment

Date of initial referral

When GP/doctor referred to neurology

Why: Calculate waiting times

Date of first clinic

First outpatient appointment

Why: Service quality indicator

Referral pathway - Local vs Non local

Local area or other region?

Why: Shows if regional center

Referral to first clinic interval

Days between referral and first clinic

Why: Key performance indicator

Duration of symptoms before intervention

Months/years with symptoms before shunt

Why: Longer duration may mean worse outcomes

Section 3: Pre-operative Clinical Assessment

Classic NPH triad documentation

Gait/Cognitive/Urinary symptoms present?

Why: Diagnosis requires these; predicts response

Pre-shunt gait assessment score

Baseline walking test (TUG, walking speed)

Why: Compare to post-op improvement

Pre-shunt cognition assessment score

MMSE, MoCA or other test score

Why: Baseline cognitive function

Urinary symptoms severity - Pre-shunt

None/Urgency/Frequency/Incontinence

Why: Baseline for improvement tracking

Comorbidities

Other conditions: HTN, diabetes, etc.

Why: Affects surgical risk

Charlston comorbidity index

Score 0-37 quantifying comorbidities

Why: Standardized measure of patient health

Previous treatments or interventions

Prior meds, physio, surgeries

Why: Shows what was tried first

Section 5: MDT & Decision Making

When case discussed at team meeting

Why: Best practice before major surgery

Specialties attending (Neurosurg, Neuro, Geri, etc.)

Why: Diverse expertise improves decisions

Why: Records clinical reasoning

Why: Captures consensus; governance

Section 8: Follow-up & Outcomes

Follow-up duration

Months from surgery to last clinic

Why: Longer follow-up = better outcome data

Number of clinics post shunt

Count outpatient appointments

Why: Resource use; complex cases need more

Time of first clinic letter post shunt

Weeks/months to first follow-up

Why: Should be 6-12 weeks; quality check

Number of follow-up visits

Total visits (clinic + emergency)

Why: Overall healthcare utilization

Number of ward attender reviews

Day unit visits for valve adjustments

Why: Need for shunt optimization

Time to symptom improvement

Weeks/months to first improvement

Why: Quick vs slow responders; predict success

Which symptom improved most

Gait/Cognition/Urinary/Equal/None?

Why: Gait usually improves most

Functional outcomes

Significant/Mild/No change/Worse?

Why: Main outcome - was surgery worthwhile?

Patient-reported outcome measures used

Which tools? NPH scale, SF-36, mRS?

Why: Standardized objective data

Post-shunt gait assessment

Repeat same test as pre-op

Why: Compare to baseline

Post-shunt cognitive assessment

Repeat cognitive test

Why: Cognition may improve slower than gait

Post-shunt urinary assessment

Resolved/Improved/Same/Worse?

Why: Complete NPH triad picture

Mobility status - aids post shunt

None/Stick/Walker/Wheelchair?

Why: Practical independence measure

Caregiver burden assessment

Did burden reduce? Yes/No/Not assessed

Why: Improvement should reduce family stress

Section 9: Imaging Follow-up

Number of radiological scans post shunt

Count CT/MRI after surgery

Why: More scans may indicate problems

First scan post shunt after discharge

How many weeks after going home?

Why: Routine 6-week scans; identify early complications

Post-shunt imaging results

Ventricles smaller/same/larger/subdural?

Why: Confirms shunt working or shows complications

Section 10: Complications & Interventions

Shunt malfunction

Did it stop working? When?

Why: Common (10-40%); affects success

Shunt infection

Any infection? When? Organism?

Why: Serious (5-15%); usually needs removal

Shunt revision reason

Overdrainage/Underdrainage/Mechanical/Infection?

Why: Identify common problems; improve practice

Re-intervention rates

How many operations after initial shunt?

Why: Multiple surgeries = complexity, cost, burden

Morbidity (subdural haematoma)

Subdural/Seizures/Stroke/Other?

Why: Safety; subdurals common with overdrainage

Shunt setting changes

Adjustments? How many? What levels?

Why: Programmable valves avoid repeat surgery

Changes in management/additional interventions

Rehab/New meds/Other procedures?

Why: Complete patient journey picture

Section 11: Final Outcome

RIP date

If died during follow-up, record date. Blank if alive

Why: Mortality data; may/may not be surgery-related
